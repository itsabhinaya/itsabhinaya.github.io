Top 4 VC's in the Chicago Area

Recently, Pitchbook released some insights into most active VC investors in the great lakes region. Out of the eight investors, four of the investors are from the Chicago area.

The time frame for all the data that is provided here is from Jan 2016 – March 2016. All of this info is available publicly on CrunchBase. So the data that was provided by Pitchbook and the info here is slightly different. I can't afford fancy databases yet.

Hyde Park Venture Partners (HPVP):

HPVP was founded in 2011 and mainly invests in seed and Series A rounds. They are focused on B2B SaaS and consumer marketplace business models. They had 25 deals in the region and On average they co-invested with five other investors.

Hyde Park Angels (HPA):

HPA is an angel group out of the Midwest, which was founded in 2007. The organization consists of all sorts of members, from executives to founders. They invest in various stages of funding in the area. They have had 19 deals to date from 2016, and on average, they co-invested with five other investors.

M25 Group (M25):

M25 is an early-stage micro-VC fund founded in 2015. They invest in a variety of industries across the Midwest, and 21 deals were made in 15 months. On average, they co-invested with three other investors.

Chicago Ventures (CV):

CV was founded in 2012 and invest in seed rounds ranging from $750K - $1M. The fund focuses on enterprise and consumer businesses, and 23 deals were made. On average, they co-invested with three other investors.

Co-Investing

If you look at the network graph, we can see that HPVP and HPA co-invested in more deals that the other two investors. Out of the 4 VCs, M25 co-invested in 3 out of 21 deals with VCs from the Chicago area. HPVP, HPA, and CV invested in the startup, Catalytic, Inc together.
